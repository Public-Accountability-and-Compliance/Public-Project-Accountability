Why Accountability Matters

When projects are paid for with public money, communities deserve transparency, fairness, and quality. Accountability isn't a burdenâ€”it's the backbone of trust, safety, and value for taxpayers.

Transparency

Clear budgets, change orders, schedules, and decisions should be available for public review.

Fairness

Competitive procurement, equal opportunity, and compliance with labor and safety laws protect workers and the public.

Quality & Safety

Projects must meet code, deliver promised outcomes, and prioritize community safety and durability.

What Public Accountability Includes

  • Open Procurement: Competitive bidding, documented evaluations, and conflict-of-interest disclosures.
  • Financial Controls: Traceable funding sources, auditable budgets, and documented change orders.
  • Compliance: Adherence to building codes, safety standards, labor laws, and contract terms.
  • Progress Reporting: Regular schedule updates, milestones, and KPI tracking accessible to stakeholders.
  • Community Engagement: Public notices, accessible meetings, and clear channels for feedback.
  • Record Retention: Keep procurement, contract, and performance documentation for auditability.

Bottom line: Accepting public funds creates obligations to the publicâ€”transparency, compliance, and performance are non-negotiable.

Transparency Checklist for Contractors

Use this quick checklist to improve accountability practices on publicly funded projects.

Before Award

  • Publish bid documents and prequalification criteria.
  • Disclose beneficial ownership and potential conflicts.
  • Define KPIs: schedule, cost, quality, safety.

During Construction

  • Maintain a public-facing dashboard with schedule and progress.
  • Document change orders with rationale and cost impact.
  • Report incidents, inspections, and compliance checks.

After Completion

  • Publish final costs vs. budget and variance analysis.
  • Provide as-builts, warranties, and O&M manuals.
  • Host a post-project lessons-learned summary.

Governance & Ethics

  • Whistleblower channels and non-retaliation policy.
  • Third-party audits and corrective action plans.
  • Data retention and public records compliance.
